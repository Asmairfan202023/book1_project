# Research Findings: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

This document will contain the research findings for the textbook chapter, including a list of peer-reviewed sources and notes on their relevance to the chapter's content.

## Peer-Reviewed Sources

1.  **Source 1**: Agrawal, A., Gans, J. S., & Goldfarb, A. (2019). *Prediction, judgment, and complexity: A theory of decision-making and artificial intelligence*. University of Chicago Press.
    *   **Relevance**: Provides a theoretical framework for understanding AI's role in decision-making, applicable to how robots reason.
2.  **Source 2**: Kober, J., Bagnell, J. A., & Peters, J. (2013). Reinforcement learning in robotics: A survey. *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, *32*(11), 1238-1274.
    *   **Relevance**: A comprehensive survey of reinforcement learning techniques used in robotics.
3.  **Source 3**: Levine, S., Finn, C., Darrell, T., & Abbeel, P. (2016). End-to-end training of deep visuomotor policies. *Journal of Machine Learning Research*, *17*(39), 1-40.
    *   **Relevance**: Foundational paper on deep learning for robotics, relevant to the NVIDIA Isaac section.
4.  **Source 4**: Maciver, M. A. (2017). Embodied intelligence. In *The Blackwell companion to consciousness* (pp. 535-551).
    *   **Relevance**: Explores the concept of embodied intelligence, central to the theme of Physical AI.
5.  **Source 5**: Quigley, M., Conley, K., Gerkey, B., Faust, J., Foote, T., Leibs, J., ... & Ng, A. Y. (2009). ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System.
    *   **Relevance**: The original paper on ROS, essential for the ROS 2 section.
6.  **Source 6**: Tan, J., Zhang, T., Coumans, E., Iscen, A., Bai, Y., Hafner, D., ... & Vanhoucke, V. (2018). Sim-to-real: Learning agile locomotion for quadruped robots. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1804.10332*.
    *   **Relevance**: Discusses the importance of simulation, relevant to the Gazebo/Unity section.
7.  **Source 7**: Tellex, S., Kollar, T., Dickerson, S., Walter, M. R., Banerjee, A. G., Teller, S., & Roy, N. (2011). Understanding natural language commands for robotic navigation and mobile manipulation. In *Twenty-fifth AAAI conference on artificial intelligence*.
    *   **Relevance**: Early work on natural language understanding for robotics, relevant to the Vision-Language-Action section.
8.  **Source 8**: Zhang, J., & Singh, S. (2018). A review of visual-lidar fusion based simultaneous localization and mapping. *Sensors*, *18*(9), 2855.
    *   **Relevance**: A review of SLAM techniques, relevant to the NVIDIA Isaac section.

## Research Notes

The selected sources cover the key areas of the textbook chapter: AI theory, reinforcement learning, deep learning in robotics, embodied intelligence, ROS, simulation, natural language processing for robotics, and SLAM. These will provide a strong foundation for the chapter's content.
